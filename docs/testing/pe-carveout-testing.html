PE Carve-Out Complex Financial Modeling Testing

Complex Financial Modeling Scenario: Distressed Conglomerate Carve-Out

In this scenario, three private equity firms (PE-A, PE-B, and PE-C) are forming a consortium to acquire a struggling division of a large conglomerate. The target division has multiple business units across different industries with varying growth profiles and capital requirements.

Key Modeling Challenges

1. Complex Acquisition Structure

The transaction involves a multi-tiered holding company structure, management rollover equity with ratchet provisions, seller financing with performance-based earnouts, and multiple co-investors with different entry valuations.

2. Sophisticated Debt Package

The financing includes senior secured term loans with mandatory prepayment provisions, second-lien debt with PIK toggle options, mezzanine financing with equity kickers, revolving credit facilities with seasonal adjustments, and vendor financing for capital expenditures.

3. Operational Restructuring

The model must address synergy modeling across business units with implementation costs, headcount reduction and facility consolidation, working capital optimization by segment, and capex rationalization with maintenance vs. growth categorization.

4. Tax Considerations

The transaction spans multiple jurisdictions with varying tax rates, involves loss carryforwards with limitations, requires transfer pricing between business units, and needs tax-efficient repatriation of cash.

5. Exit Strategy Planning

The model needs to evaluate multiple exit scenarios (IPO, strategic sale, secondary), consider division-by-division or whole company exit options, calculate sponsor-specific returns with carried interest waterfall, and model management equity value under various exit scenarios.

What to Look For in Add-in Responses

When evaluating the add-in's performance on these test cases, look for:

  • Ability to understand complex financial structures and relationships
  • Proper implementation of financial modeling best practices
  • Correct handling of tiered capital structures and returns waterfall calculations
  • Understanding of PE transaction mechanics and terminology
  • Appropriate organization of multi-tab models with clear data flow
  • Implementation of proper sensitivity analyses and scenario modeling

Complex Acquisition Structure Modeling

These tests evaluate the add-in's ability to help build complex acquisition structures with multiple investors and tiered holding companies.

# Test Query Result Feedback
1 Create a worksheet for modeling a multi-tier holding company structure with 3 PE sponsors having different ownership percentages (40%, 35%, 25%) and management rollover equity of 10%
2 Design a formula for calculating management equity ratchet that increases ownership by 5% if EBITDA exceeds $100M and another 5% if it exceeds $150M
3 Create a model for an earnout provision where the seller receives additional $50M if revenue CAGR exceeds 15% over the first 3 years
4 Model a co-investment mechanism where investors A, B, and C have different entry valuations (10x, 11x, and 12x EBITDA) and calculate the implied blended purchase multiple
5 Create a sources and uses of funds table for a $750M acquisition with 50% equity contribution, senior debt at 4x EBITDA, and the remainder in mezzanine financing

Sophisticated Debt Package Modeling

These tests evaluate the add-in's ability to model complex debt structures with multiple tranches and unique features.

# Test Query Result Feedback
1 Create a debt schedule for a senior secured term loan with 1% quarterly amortization, 5% interest rate, and mandatory prepayments of 50% of excess cash flow
2 Model a second-lien debt with PIK toggle options that allows the company to pay 50% of interest in additional debt if cash flow coverage ratio falls below 1.2x
3 Create a model for mezzanine financing with a 12% coupon and equity kickers worth 2% of equity upon exit if IRR exceeds 20%
4 Model a revolving credit facility with seasonal adjustments that increases availability to $100M during peak season (Q4) and reduces to $60M during other quarters
5 Design a debt covenant compliance model that tracks and calculates leverage ratio, interest coverage ratio, fixed charge coverage ratio, and minimum EBITDA tests

Operational Restructuring Modeling

# Test Query Result Feedback

3 Model working capital optimization by segment with different DSO, DPO, and inventory turnover targets and transition periods to achieve them
4 Create a capex rationalization model that categorizes spending as maintenance vs. growth and optimizes allocation across 4 business units based on ROI thresholds
5 Build a SKU rationalization model that analyzes profitability by product and eliminates bottom 20% of products while modeling revenue retention of 85%

Exit Strategy Modeling

These tests evaluate the add-in's ability to model complex exit scenarios and returns calculations for different investor classes.

# Test Query Result Feedback
1 Create a model for multiple exit scenarios: IPO at 10x EBITDA, strategic sale at 12x EBITDA, and secondary sale to another PE firm at 9x EBITDA
2 Model division-by-division exit options with Business Unit A sold in Year 3, Business Unit B in Year 4, and remaining business in Year 5
3 Create a sponsor-specific returns model with carried interest waterfall (8% hurdle, 20% carry) and calculation of returns for each PE firm
4 Design a management equity value model under various exit scenarios with vesting schedules and different performance thresholds
5 Build a sensitivity analysis showing exit multiples (8x-14x) vs. hold periods (3-7 years) and the resulting IRR and MOIC for all investor classes
